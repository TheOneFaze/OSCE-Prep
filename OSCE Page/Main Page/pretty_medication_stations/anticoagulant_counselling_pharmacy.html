High-Yield Notes Full Script

High-Yield OSCE Points 🧠

🩺 Set up

  • 🧼 Wash hands and don PPE if appropriate
  • 👋 Introduce yourself (name, role)
  • 🆔 Confirm patient's name and date of birth
  • 📋 Explain purpose: discuss anticoagulant medication (apixaban)
  • ✅ Gain consent to proceed

🤔 Understanding & ICE

  • 💬 Explore what patient knows about atrial fibrillation and clot risk
  • ❓ Ask about concerns or expectations regarding anticoagulation
  • 🙋 Encourage questions and active participation

🩸 Medication Purpose

  • 💊 Apixaban is a blood thinner to prevent clots caused by AF
  • 📅 Taken lifelong unless advised otherwise
  • ⏰ Taken twice daily, 5 mg per dose, 12 hours apart

🍽️ How to Take

  • 🍔 Can be taken with or without food
  • 🚫 Do not miss doses, or double dose if missed

⚠️ Side Effects & Safety

  • 📈 Increased bleeding risk: bruising, nosebleeds, bleeding gums
  • 🤢 Possible nausea, skin reactions, anemia
  • 🩸 Minor cuts may bleed longer (~10 minutes)
  • ibuprofen unless approved
  • 🍷 Limit alcohol to reduce bleeding risk

🚨 Safety Netting

  • 🆘 Seek urgent help if severe bleeding, blood in urine/stool, unexplained bruising, or head injury
  • 👨‍⚕️ Inform healthcare professionals (dentist, surgeon) before procedures
  • 🧤 Use electric razors, wear gloves during risky activities

🧪 Monitoring

  • 🔬 GP will arrange occasional blood tests to monitor safety
  • 💳 Carry anticoagulant alert information at all times

Full Word-for-Word OSCE Script 📜

🩺 Set up

"Hi, I’m [Your Name], a pharmacy student. I’ll wash my hands and put on PPE if needed. Can I please confirm your full name and date of birth?"

"You’ve been prescribed apixaban, which is an anticoagulant — a blood thinner. Today, I’d like to explain how it works, how to take it safely, and what to watch out for. Is that okay with you?"

🩸 What is Apixaban?

"Apixaban works by reducing your blood’s ability to clot, which helps prevent harmful blood clots. This is especially important if you have atrial fibrillation — an irregular heart rhythm that can cause clots to form and lead to strokes or heart attacks."

📆 How to Take Apixaban

"You should take apixaban twice daily, 5 milligrams each dose, about 12 hours apart. Try to take it at the same times every day. If you forget a dose, take it as soon as you remember unless it’s nearly time for your next dose — don’t double up."

"You can take it with or without food."

"Most patients take this medication for life unless their doctor tells them otherwise."

⚠️ Side Effects and Safety

"Apixaban increases your risk of bleeding. You may bruise more easily or have nosebleeds or bleeding gums. Minor cuts or scrapes might bleed longer — around 10 minutes — before they stop."

"Common side effects include nausea, skin reactions, or anemia."

"Avoid medications like ibuprofen or other NSAIDs unless your doctor says it’s okay, as these can increase bleeding risk. Also limit your alcohol intake."

🚨 Important Safety Advice

"Always tell your pharmacist you’re taking apixaban before buying any over-the-counter medicines."

"Inform any healthcare professional, including dentists or surgeons, before you have any procedures."

"For lifestyle, using an electric razor for shaving and wearing gloves during activities like gardening can help reduce your risk of cuts and bruises."

"If you notice severe bleeding, blood in your urine or stools, unexplained bruising, or head injury, seek urgent medical help."

🧪 Monitoring

"Your GP will arrange blood tests now and then to check your kidney and liver function to make sure the medicine is still safe for you."

✅ Summary

"So, to sum up: take apixaban twice daily at 5 mg per dose, watch out for bleeding risks, inform healthcare professionals before procedures, and don’t miss your doses. Do you have any questions or concerns about your medication?"
