  • 🔬 Overgrowth of normal vaginal bacteria
  • 👃 Causes thin, grey-white discharge + fishy smell

Full Word-for-Word OSCE Script 📜

🩺 Introduction

"Hi, I’m [Your Name], a medical student. I understand you were recently seen in clinic for a change in vaginal discharge. Before we go further, could I confirm your name and date of birth?"

"I’d like to explain the results and what it means going forward. Is that okay?"

🔍 Understanding & Concerns

"Have you heard of bacterial vaginosis before?"

"Do you have any concerns — for example, about it being a sexually transmitted infection or affecting fertility?"

🧫 What is Bacterial Vaginosis?

"Bacterial vaginosis, or BV, is a common cause of vaginal discharge. It’s not a sexually transmitted infection, but is linked to changes in the balance of natural bacteria in the vagina."

"There’s usually a healthy balance of bacteria, but in BV one type overgrows — leading to symptoms like thin, greyish discharge and a fishy odour, especially after sex."

⚠️ Important Reassurance

"It’s not dangerous and doesn’t usually cause serious problems. However, in pregnancy or before procedures it can be more relevant. It doesn’t cause infertility."

💊 Treatment

"We treat it with an antibiotic called metronidazole — either as a tablet or a gel you insert into the vagina. It usually clears up within a week."

"While being treated, it’s best to avoid alcohol if taking tablets, as it may cause nausea. Avoid using vaginal washes, douching, or perfumed products going forward — these upset the balance further."

🔁 Recurrence

"BV can come back, especially after your period or sex. It’s not harmful but if it recurs frequently we can discuss options like longer treatments or specialist referral."

✅ Summary & Safety Net

"So to summarise — BV is a common bacterial imbalance. It’s easily treatable, not an STI, and doesn’t affect fertility. We recommend treatment, avoiding vaginal products, and seeing a doctor if symptoms persist or return."

"Do you have any questions or worries I can help with?"

"Here’s a leaflet with everything we discussed and advice on how to prevent recurrence. Thanks for your time."
