🧴 Explaining a Diagnosis of Eczema
High-Yield Notes Full Script

High-Yield OSCE Points 🧠

🩺 Set up

  • 🧼 Wash hands and PPE
  • 👋 Introduce name and role
  • 🆔 Confirm patient's name and DOB
  • 📋 Explain purpose of discussion
  • 🗺️ Use signposting: "We’ll start by discussing what eczema is, then talk through management."

🤔 Understanding & Concerns

  • ❓ Ask “What do you know about eczema?”
  • 💬 Use ICE (Ideas, Concerns, Expectations)

📖 Explanation of Eczema

  • 🧬 Chronic inflammatory skin condition
  • 📉 Symptoms: dry, itchy, red skin; flares
  • ⚠️ Common triggers: soaps, allergens, stress
  • 🚫 Not contagious

💊 Management

  • 🧴 Emollients: daily use to restore skin barrier
  • 💊 Topical steroids for flares (appropriate strength per site)
  • 🔁 Identify and avoid triggers (soaps, allergens, stress)
  • 📋 Written eczema action plan
  • 💉 Flu vaccine if severe or recurrent infections

👋 Closing

  • 📄 Offer leaflet/info
  • 🙋 Ask for questions
  • 🩺 Arrange follow-up if needed
  • 🚨 Safety-net: infection signs (crusting, oozing) → GP

Full Word-for-Word OSCE Script 📜

🩺 Set up

“Hi, my name is [Your Name], I’m a medical student. I’ve been asked to talk to you about the diagnosis from your recent dermatology appointment.”

“To begin, can I confirm your full name and date of birth?”

“Today we’ll discuss what eczema is, how it can affect you, and what we can do to manage it. Sound good?”

🤔 Understanding & Concerns

“What do you already know about eczema?”

“Do you have any specific worries or expectations today?”

🧴 What is Eczema?

“Eczema is a long-term condition where your skin becomes inflamed. This causes dry, red, itchy patches that can flare up periodically.”

“It’s not contagious. Triggers include soaps, allergens, and stress. Everyone has slightly different triggers.”

💊 Management Options

“The foundation of treatment is using moisturisers — called emollients — regularly to restore the skin barrier.”

“During flares, steroid creams help reduce inflammation. The strength depends on the affected area and severity.”

“Avoiding known triggers and managing itching helps prevent damage to the skin and reduces risk of infection.”

“We’ll also give you a written eczema action plan to help you recognise flares and know what to do.”

📎 Summary & Safety Netting

“To summarise: eczema is a common and manageable condition. Daily moisturisers and short courses of steroids are key.”

“If your skin shows signs of infection — like oozing, crusting, or worsening redness — please contact your GP.”

“Here’s a leaflet with further information. Do you have any questions for me?”

“Thank you for your time today!”
