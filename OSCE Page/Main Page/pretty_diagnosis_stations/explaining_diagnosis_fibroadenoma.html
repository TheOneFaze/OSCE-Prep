🎀 Explaining a Diagnosis of Fibroadenoma
High-Yield Notes Full Script

High-Yield OSCE Points 🧠

🩺 Set up

  • 🧼 Wash hands and PPE
  • 👩 Introduce self, name, role
  • 🆔 Confirm patient identity
  • 🗣️ Clarify consultation purpose
  • 📁 Aware of background: first triple assessment, first presentation

🤔 Understanding & Concerns

  • 💭 Ask what patient knows about breast lumps or fibroadenomas
  • 😟 Explore concerns, especially about cancer
  • 💬 Use ICE framework (Ideas, Concerns, Expectations)

📖 Explanation

  • 🎀 Fibroadenomas = benign (non-cancerous) breast lumps
  • 👩‍⚕️ Common in women under 30
  • 🧪 Diagnosed with triple assessment

💊 Management

  • 🩺 Often no treatment needed; monitor only
  • 🔪 Removal if large, painful, growing, or by choice

❤️ Empathy & Support

  • 💖 Demonstrate empathy throughout
  • 🧠 Explore emotional response to information
  • 📎 Provide leaflet/resources
  • 🚨 Safety netting: advise return if changes develop

👋 Closing

  • 🧠 Check understanding via patient summary
  • 🤝 Shared decision making
  • 📅 Discuss follow-up
  • ❓ Address final questions, thank patient

Full Word-for-Word OSCE Script 📜

🩺 Introduction

“Hi, I’m [Your Name], a medical student. I’ve been asked to talk to you about your recent breast clinic visit. Can I confirm your name and date of birth?”

“Today, I’ll explain the results and answer your questions. Is that okay?”

📖 Background

“I understand you’ve had a triple assessment for a lump in your breast — and this is your first time being investigated for something like this, is that right?”

🤔 Understanding & Concerns

“Can I ask, what do you already know about the lump?”

“Do you have any concerns, particularly about it being cancer?”

🎀 What is a Fibroadenoma?

“A fibroadenoma is a benign (non-cancerous) breast lump. It doesn’t increase your risk of cancer.”

“It’s like a collection of normal breast tissue that’s just grown differently — almost like a breast mole. They’re most common in younger women under 30.”

🔬 How was it Diagnosed?

“You’ve had a triple assessment: a clinical exam, an ultrasound/mammogram, and possibly a biopsy. These confirmed it’s a fibroadenoma.”

🛠️ Management Options

“In most cases, we just monitor it — especially if it’s not causing issues.”

“If it becomes painful, grows, or you’d like it removed, a minor operation is possible.”

❤️ Empathy & Support

“How are you feeling about everything we’ve discussed so far?”

“It’s completely normal to feel anxious even when the news is reassuring.”

📎 Summary & Safety Netting

“So to summarise, you have a benign fibroadenoma. No treatment is needed unless it causes problems.”

“We’ll give you a leaflet, and if anything changes — like a new lump, pain, or skin changes — please come back.”

✅ Final Check

“Just to make sure I’ve explained everything clearly, could you summarise in your own words what you’ve understood?”

“Do you have any other questions?”

“Would you like anyone else to be in the room or speak to someone further?”

“Thanks very much for your time!”
