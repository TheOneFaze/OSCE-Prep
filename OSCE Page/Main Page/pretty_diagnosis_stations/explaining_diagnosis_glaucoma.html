High-Yield OSCE Points 🧠

🩺 Introduction

  • 🧼 Wash hands + PPE
  • 👋 Introduce self and role
  • 🆔 Confirm patient details
  • 📋 Explain purpose of consultation

🤔 Understanding & Concerns

  • ❓ Ask what they know about glaucoma
  • 💬 Use ICE framework

📖 Explanation of Glaucoma

  • 👁️ Group of conditions causing optic nerve damage
  • 📈 Often related to high eye pressure
  • 🧓 Common in older adults, often asymptomatic initially
  • ⚠️ If untreated, can cause gradual vision loss

💊 Management

  • 💧 Eye drops to lower pressure
  • 🔬 Regular monitoring (visual fields, OCT)
  • 🔪 Laser or surgical options if severe

👋 Closing

  • 📄 Offer leaflet
  • 🙋 Check for final questions
  • 📆 Reinforce importance of follow-up

Full Word-for-Word OSCE Script 📜

🩺 Introduction

“Hello, I’m [Your Name], a medical student. I’ve been asked to explain your recent eye clinic findings. Could I confirm your full name and date of birth?”

🤔 Understanding & Concerns

“Have you heard of glaucoma before?”

“What concerns do you have after your eye test?”

👁️ What is Glaucoma?

“Glaucoma is an eye condition where increased pressure or other factors lead to damage of the optic nerve — the cable that connects your eye to your brain.”

“It usually develops slowly and doesn’t cause symptoms early on, which is why it’s often picked up during eye tests.”

⚠️ Consequences & Diagnosis

“If untreated, glaucoma can gradually lead to peripheral vision loss and potentially blindness. But with treatment, we can prevent this.”

“We diagnose it using tests like eye pressure measurement, visual field testing, and optic nerve scans.”

💧 Treatment

“The most common treatment is eye drops that reduce the pressure inside your eye.”

“There are also laser procedures or surgery if drops don’t work or if the glaucoma is more advanced.”

📆 Long-term Plan

“You’ll have regular follow-up at the eye clinic to monitor for any changes and adjust treatment if needed.”

✅ Summary & Reassurance

“So in summary, you’ve got early glaucoma, which we can manage. The key is regular monitoring and taking your drops as prescribed.”

“Here’s some written information about the condition. Do you have any questions?”

“Thank you for coming in today!”
