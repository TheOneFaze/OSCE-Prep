🩸 Explaining a Diagnosis of Type 1 Diabetes

High-Yield Notes Full Script

High-Yield OSCE Points 🧠

Introduction

  • 🧼 Wash hands + PPE
  • 👋 Introduce name + role
  • 🆔 Confirm patient identity
  • 📋 Clarify purpose of consultation

Understanding & Concerns

  • 💬 Ask “What do you know about type 1 diabetes?”
  • ❓ Use ICE to explore concerns & expectations

Explanation

  • 🧬 Autoimmune condition – body attacks insulin-producing cells
  • 🩸 Causes high blood sugar levels (hyperglycaemia)
  • 📅 Usually diagnosed in childhood or adolescence
  • ⚠️ Without treatment → DKA, long-term complications

Management

  • 💉 Insulin injections or pump therapy
  • 🍽️ Diet & carbohydrate counting
  • 📱 Regular blood glucose monitoring (finger-prick or CGM)
  • 🩺 Diabetes clinic support & education

Closing

  • 📄 Offer info leaflet
  • 🙋 Invite questions
  • 📞 Safety net + support services

Full Word-for-Word OSCE Script 📜

🩺 Introduction

“Hello, I’m [Your Name], a medical student. I’ve been asked to explain the recent diagnosis you received. Could I first confirm your name and date of birth?”

“Today we’ll go over what type 1 diabetes is and what it means for you.”

🔍 Understanding & Concerns

“Have you heard of type 1 diabetes before?”

“Do you have any worries or expectations you’d like me to address?”

🧬 What is Type 1 Diabetes?

“Type 1 diabetes is an autoimmune condition where your immune system attacks the insulin-producing cells in your pancreas.”

“Insulin is a hormone that helps sugar enter your body’s cells for energy. Without it, sugar builds up in your blood, which can be dangerous.”

⚠️ Symptoms & Diagnosis

“People often present with symptoms like thirst, tiredness, frequent urination, and weight loss. We confirmed the diagnosis based on your blood glucose levels and other tests.”

💉 Management

“The main treatment is insulin — either through multiple daily injections or an insulin pump.”

“You’ll also need to check your blood sugar regularly. We’ll teach you how to adjust insulin based on your readings, meals, and activity.”

📚 Education & Support

“You’ll be supported by a specialist diabetes team — including doctors, nurses, and dietitians. You’ll be offered structured education sessions to help you manage the condition confidently.”

✅ Summary

“So to summarise, type 1 diabetes is a lifelong condition that’s managed well with insulin and monitoring. We’ll provide full support to help you adjust.”

“Do you have any questions about what we’ve covered?”

“Thanks for your time — here’s a leaflet with more details and the contact for your diabetes nurse.”

Opening the consultation

Washes hands and dons PPE if appropriate
