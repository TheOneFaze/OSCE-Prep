üìö Knowledge

  • Colonoscopy uses a thin, flexible tube with a camera inserted through the bottom to examine the entire colon.
  • Air is insufflated to expand the colon for better visualization, may cause bloating or urge to defecate.
  • Procedure takes approximately 30-45 minutes.
  • Patient must have an empty bowel, achieved by laxatives or enemas prior to procedure.
  • The at-home fecal immunochemical test (FIT) screens for hidden blood, indicating need for colonoscopy.
  • Common causes of blood in stool: bowel cancer, ulcers, diverticular disease, hemorrhoids, inflammatory bowel disease, polyps.
  • Biopsies may be taken if abnormalities found; results take several weeks.
  • Complications include bloating, cramps, small amounts of bleeding; rare risks include bowel perforation and infection.
  • Urgent advice if severe bleeding, pain, fever, or vomiting occurs post-procedure.

‚úÖ Strategy & Summary

  • Explain the procedure and preparation clearly.
  • Reassure about sedation and pain management.
  • Discuss the significance of findings and biopsy.
  • Warn about complications and appropriate follow-up.
  • Confirm patient understanding and address concerns.
