üìö Knowledge

  • ERCP = Endoscopic Retrograde Cholangiopancreatography
  • Used to diagnose and treat conditions of the bile ducts, pancreas, and gallbladder.
  • Involves camera insertion via the mouth down to the duodenum with X-ray dye injection.
  • Commonly used to remove gallstones causing pancreatitis.
  • Usually performed under sedation or general anaesthetic.
  • Requires fasting for 6 hours before the procedure.
  • Average procedure time is ~30 minutes.
  • Risks: bloating, sore throat, pancreatitis, bleeding, infection, or duct access failure.
  • Post-procedure observation for ~6 hours before discharge.

  • Clarify what ERCP is and why it's done.
  • Explain the route of the camera, anaesthesia, and need to fast.
  • Reassure about pain management and safety.
  • Mention recovery and risk monitoring.
  • Check patient understanding and address concerns.
