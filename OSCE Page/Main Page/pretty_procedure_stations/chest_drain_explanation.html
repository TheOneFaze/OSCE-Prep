High-Yield OSCE Points üß†

üìö Knowledge

  • A chest drain is a tube inserted between the ribs into the pleural space to remove air or fluid.
  • Local anaesthetic is used; additional pain relief can be given.
  • Ultrasound guidance is used for accurate placement.
  • The tube is connected to a drainage system to allow lung re-expansion.
  • The drain usually stays 1-2 days but can be longer.
  • Risks include pain, bleeding, infection, damage to organs, and tube dislodgement.
  • Removal is a quick procedure, may cause mild discomfort.
  • Patients must stay in hospital while the drain is in place.

‚úÖ Strategy & summary

  • Explain the procedure clearly, including pain control and duration.
  • Discuss common risks and reassure about monitoring.
  • Explain the drain removal process.
  • Check patient understanding and willingness to proceed.
