üìö Knowledge

  • TB is a serious bacterial disease primarily affecting the lungs but can spread to other organs.
  • Treatment is essential to prevent complications and disease spread.
  • Standard treatment lasts ~6 months using 4 drugs: rifampicin, isoniazid, pyrazinamide, ethambutol.
  • Initial 2 months: all four drugs; subsequent 4 months: rifampicin and isoniazid only.
  • Public health notification is required; close contacts may need screening.
  • Common side effects include orange bodily fluids (rifampicin), neuropathy (isoniazid), joint pain and liver toxicity (pyrazinamide), vision problems (ethambutol).
  • Monitoring includes liver function tests and vision assessments.
  • Patients are infectious for approximately 2 weeks after starting treatment; self-isolation is advised.
  • Adherence to medication is critical to prevent drug resistance and treatment failure.

‚úÖ Strategy & Summary

  • Ensure patient understands treatment duration and drug regimen.
  • Explain side effects and importance of monitoring.
  • Highlight infection control measures including isolation and contact precautions.
  • Encourage adherence and clarify infectious period.
