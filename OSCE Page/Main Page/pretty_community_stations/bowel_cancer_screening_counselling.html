  • Crucially, correct misconceptions: Explain they have been invited because they are now 60, not because they are considered high-risk or likely have cancer.
  • Explain the programme is offered to everyone aged 60-74 in England.
  • Explain the main purpose of screening: to find and remove pre-cancerous growths (polyps) to prevent cancer, or to find cancer at an early, more treatable stage.

ðŸ§ª The At-Home Kit (FIT test)

  • Describe the simple, non-invasive process: collecting a small poo sample at home and posting it to a lab.
  • Explain that the test looks for tiny, invisible amounts of blood in the poo.
  • Reassure that a positive test does not mean cancer; there are many other causes of blood, such as polyps or haemorrhoids.

  • Normal Result: No further action is needed until the next routine kit in 2 years.
  • Abnormal Result: Leads to an invitation for a colonoscopy to investigate the cause of the blood.
  • Explain what a colonoscopy is: a thin, flexible tube with a camera to look inside the bowel.
  • Reassure that options for sedation and pain relief are available to ensure comfort.

  • Summarise the key points, re-emphasising that screening is a routine, preventative measure for their age group.
  • Use the "teach-back" method to ensure they have understood and to correct any lingering misconceptions (e.g., "So just to check I've explained it well...").
  • Briefly mention key red flag symptoms to look out for (e.g., visible blood, change in bowel habit, weight loss).
